Her work is committed to representing the vibrant composition of energies and women in their natural habitat.

In 2023, Nikita completed her Art training in figurative painting and abstraction at the Art Academy London, to further refine her style and conformation.

Her artistic approach is largely influenced by the likes of Tamara De Lempicka, Georgia Oâ€™Keeffe and Damien Hirst. She is fascinated by anatomy and the mystery and movement of abstract forces.

Channeling her emotions into both her color palette and work, Nikita lends wisdom to the large and expressive brush strokes that act as a reflection of her own personal identity. They serve as a tool to demonstrate powerful other-worldly energies. Her use of juxtaposition of light and dark, coupled with textural abstraction, are employed as boundaries to represent cosmic forces and their connectivity to water.

Through figurative paintings, Nikita explores the nuances of identity and color. She enjoys forging a connection between female subjects and their surroundings, seeking to explore the ongoing kinship between humankind and earthly realms.

When not working from her home studio in London, Nikita travels frequently in search of inspiration, translating her experiences through vibrant perspectives on art, born through a fascination of vivid color placement and articulate mark-making.
